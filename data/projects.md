This project focused on the analysis of whole-proteome and post-translational modification (PTM) proteomics data in Saccharomyces cerevisiae, with a particular emphasis on methylation. The study included three experimental conditions: a wild-type control and two separate mutant conditions targeting key methyl-donor genes.
We developed an R-based analysis pipeline to process PEAKS output generated by a university proteomics core facility. The pipeline performed data cleaning, annotation, PTM identification, and differential analyses of protein abundance and PTMs across experimental conditions. To further contextualize these findings, we conducted pathway analysis and gene ontology enrichment analyses.
The final stage of the project involved generating an interactive HTML report that summarized the data cleaning workflow, analytical methods, and key results, enabling researchers to more easily explore and interpret the findings.
